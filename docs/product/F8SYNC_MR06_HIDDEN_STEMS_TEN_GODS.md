## 3. Ten Gods Derivation

Ten Gods (十神) describe the relationship between the Day Master stem and every other stem in the chart.

### 3.1 The Ten Gods Table

Relationship is determined by: element relationship (same/produces/controls/produced by/controlled by) × polarity (same/different).

| Relationship | Same Polarity | Different Polarity |
|---|---|---|
| Same element | 比肩 Bi Jian (Rob Wealth) | 劫財 Jie Cai (Friend) |
| Day Master produces | 食神 Shi Shen (Eating God) | 傷官 Shang Guan (Hurting Officer) |
| Day Master controls | 偏財 Pian Cai (Indirect Wealth) | 正財 Zheng Cai (Direct Wealth) |
| Produces Day Master | 偏印 Pian Yin (Indirect Resource) | 正印 Zheng Yin (Direct Resource) |
| Controls Day Master | 七殺 Qi Sha (Seven Killings) | 正官 Zheng Guan (Direct Officer) |

Confidence: **HIGH** — universal agreement.

### 3.2 Primary Stem for Ten God Calculation

When calculating Ten Gods for a Branch, the **main hidden stem** is used as the primary representative of that branch.

Mid and residual stems are calculated separately and listed as secondary Ten Gods within the branch.

## 4. Proposed Rules

### Rule MR-06-A: Hidden Stems

```
Each Earthly Branch contains 1–3 hidden Heavenly Stems as defined
in the MR-06 hidden stems table.

The main stem is the primary energy of the branch.
Mid and residual stems are secondary energies.
```

Confidence: HIGH

### Rule MR-06-B: Ten Gods Derivation

```
Ten Gods are derived from the relationship between the Day Master stem
and every other Heavenly Stem in the chart (including hidden stems).

Relationship is determined by element interaction × polarity as defined
in the MR-06 Ten Gods table.
```

Confidence: HIGH

### Rule MR-06-C: Branch Representative Stem

```
For Ten God calculation, the main hidden stem of a branch is used
as the primary representative of that branch.

Mid and residual stems produce secondary Ten Gods and are listed separately.
```

Confidence: HIGH
